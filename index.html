About Leukemia

Leukemia is the most common cancer in children and teens, accounting for almost 1 out of 3 cancers. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

Acute lymphocytic leukemia (ALL) is the most common type, followed by acute myeloid leukemia (AML). :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

Treatment Options

The main treatment for most childhood leukemias is chemotherapy. For some children with higher-risk leukemias, high-dose chemotherapy may be given along with a stem cell transplant. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

Other treatments might also be used in special circumstances. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}
